            </div>
            <div class="col-sm-10 standard-gap--mobile-only">
                <h2>Archaeopteryx</h2>
                <p>All described specimens of Sinosauropteryx preserve integumentary structures (filaments arising from the skin) which most palaeontologists interpret as very primitive type of feathers. These short, down-like filaments are preserved along the back half of the skull, the arms, neck, back, and top and bottom of the tail. </p>
                <ul>
                    <li>Despite its feathers, most palaeontologists do not consider Sinosauropteryx to be a bird.</li>
                    <li>There is only one named species of Sinosauropteryx, S. prima.</li>
                </ul>
            </div>
        </div>
